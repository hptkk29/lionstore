Social media login

Follow these steps to allow your customers to sign in using their social media accounts. Right now only Facebook, Google and Twitter are supported, but if you want another one, just contact me and I will be glad to help you

1. Facebook

- First sign in to your Facebook account and then go to developers page https://developers.facebook.com/apps

- Then click "Create App" green button as shown in this picture:

- Then a new popup will show to select type of app, select Consumer and click "Continue"

- Now enter your app name and email address and also business account if exists and click "Continue"

- Once your app is created, go to left sidebar and select "Facebook Login" => "Settings"

- Now if you saw this red error click "Get Advanced Access"

- Now we need access to two options "public_profile" and "email" as you can see below

- After click on each button and confirm access

- Now let's get back to "Facebook Login" => "Settings" and add redirect url as you can see in the photo below, change only "your-domain.com" and keep "/auth/facebook/callback"

- After that you will see a message above tells you to complete Data Use Checkup, click "Get Started"

- And then check all checkboxes and click "Continue" then "Submit"

- Now go to "Settings" => "Basic"

- Then enter all the data you can see below like "Privacy Policy URL", "Terms page" "App Logo"...

- After that you are ready to change to live mode and getting started

- And finally copy your app id and app secret to your dashboard then auth settings
https://your-domain.com/dashboard/settings/auth

2. Twitter

- Sign in to your Twitter account and then go to developer page https://developer.twitter.com/en/portal/projects-and-apps and click "Create app"

- Choose your app name and click "Next"

- Now you can see your app key and app secret copy them to your dashboard => auth settings
https://your-domain.com/dashboard/settings/auth

- Now go to your app settings and choose "Authentication settings" then click "Edit"

- Check all and enter callback url change only "your-domain.com" and edit all fields then click "Save"

3. Google

- Once you’re logged in with Google, open the Google Developers console. That should open up the Google Dashboard page, as shown in the following screenshot.

- From the top-left menu, click on the Select a project link. That should open up a popup, as shown in the following screenshot and click "NEW PROJECT".

- Enter project name and click "Create"

- You will be redirected to the Dashboard page. Click on the Credentials from the left sidebar, and click Configure consent screen button

- Choose "External User Type" and click "Create"

- Now provide a name and email for your app and enter all required data in this page and click "Save and Continue"

- And now click on "Add or Remove Scopes"

- A popup will show, select like this screenshot and scroll down then click Update after that click "Save and Continue"

- Next click Save and Continue

- navigate to the “Credentials” tab in the sidebar. Under “Create Credentials”, select “OAuth client ID”.

- Select "Web application" and enter any name then enter your domain url and callback url, change only your-domain.com

- Now you can see your app id and secret key, go to your dashboard and then auth settings and put them
https://your-domain.com/dashboard/settings/auth
